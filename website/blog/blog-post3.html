Healthy Eating on a Budget

Eating healthy can often be perceived as expensive, but it doesn't have to be. With some simple tips and planning, it's possible to eat nutritious and delicious meals on a budget. Here are some small and fast tips to help you eat healthy on a budget:

  • Plan your meals ahead of time to avoid impulsive purchases and food waste.
  • Buy in-season fruits and vegetables, which are often cheaper and fresher.
  • Shop at discount grocery stores, such as Aldi or Lidl, for affordable produce, grains, and proteins.
  • Buy in bulk, especially for dry goods like beans, rice, and oats, to save money in the long run.
  • Compare prices and look for sales or deals before making purchases.
  • Cook at home instead of eating out to save money and have more control over ingredients and portions.
  • Use frozen fruits and vegetables, which are just as nutritious as fresh ones and often cheaper.

By following these tips, you can eat healthy without breaking the bank. Here are three meal ideas that apply these tips:

Meal 1: Vegetarian Stir-Fry

  • 1 cup brown rice (cooked)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 tablespoon low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped peanuts (optional)

Directions:

  1. Cook rice according to package instructions.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and garlic and cook until soft.
  3. Add bell pepper, zucchini, and broccoli and cook until vegetables are tender.
  4. Stir in cooked rice, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Cook for a few more minutes until heated through.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with chopped peanuts if desired.

Meal 2: Turkey Chili

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 ounces)
  • 1 can kidney beans, rinsed and drained (15 ounces)
  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained (15 ounces)
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheese, sour cream, green onions

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the ground turkey over medium heat until browned.
  2. Add the onion and garlic, and cook until the onion is softened.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes, kidney beans, black beans, corn, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Stir to combine.
  4. Add the water and bring the chili to a simmer.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and let the chili simmer for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Serve hot with your choice of toppings.

Meal 2: Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 1 sweet potato, peeled and chopped
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 small corn tortillas

Optional toppings:

  • Avocado
  • Salsa
  • Greek yogurt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Toss the sweet potato with a little bit of olive oil, salt, and pepper, and spread it out on a baking sheet. Roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes, or until tender.
  3. In a pan over medium heat, sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant. Add the black beans, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper, and cook until heated through.
  4. Warm the corn tortillas in a separate pan or in the microwave.
  5. To assemble the tacos, divide the sweet potato and black bean mixture among the tortillas. Top with avocado, salsa, and Greek yogurt, if desired.
